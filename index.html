ZENG LAB · Computational Epidemiology & Proteomics

We study data-driven mechanisms linking lifestyle, multi-omics signals, and neurodegenerative diseases. Our work spans robust causal triangulation, transfer learning, and high-dimensional mediation.

ZENG LAB

We build reliable statistical and machine-learning methods to translate multi-omics signals into actionable population health insights.

Mission

Advance robust epidemiology by integrating proteomics with causal designs. We emphasize transparent methodology, reproducible code, and clinically meaningful predictions.

RESEARCH

From principled weighting to transfer learning, we design methods that travel across populations without losing validity.

Proteomic risk modeling for PD in hearing-loss populations

We screen proteomic biomarkers in a general cohort, then adapt models to hearing-loss subgroups via density-ratio reweighting and similarity-regularized transfer learning.

Elastic Net Cox Re-weighted survival Transfer learning

High-dimensional mediation frameworks

We develop scalable mediation pipelines linking lifestyle → proteome → PD, integrating MR-style instruments and composite-null testing for robust interpretation.

Mediation MR + Omics Causal triangulation
